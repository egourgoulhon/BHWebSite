Geometry and physics of black holes

These pages are devoted to Éric Gourgoulhon's lectures at various places:

  • PSL graduate program in Physics, ENS, Paris, France, April-June 2025
  • Institut Henri Poincaré, Paris, France, 18-21 March 2024
  • Albert Einstein Institute, Potsdam, Germany, 6-7 December 2023
  • PSL graduate programs in Astrophysics and in Physics, ENS, Paris, France, May-June 2023
  • School on Black Holes and Gravitational Waves, Chennai, India, January 2022
  • Gravitational Waves 2018 Summer School, Les Houches, France, July 2018
  • Dubna International Advanced School of Theoretical Physics, Dubna, Russia, May 2017
  • Chaire Georges Lemaître, UCLouvain, Louvain-la-Neuve, Belgium, November 2016
  • Advanced Lectures of Institut d'Astrophysique de Paris, Paris, France, March-April 2016

The lectures introduce some aspects of black hole physics. The prerequisite is some basic knowledge of general relativity, at the level of an introductory course.

You will find here:

  • the lecture notes, as well as their sources (LaTeX, Inkscape and SageMath files)
  • the slides and videos of the lectures
  • some SageMath notebooks for computations involved in the course
